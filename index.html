Understanding EV Charging

Essential information to help you make informed decisions

Charging Levels Explained

  • Level 1: Standard 120V outlet, 3-5 miles/hour (home use)
  • Level 2: 240V, 12-80 miles/hour (most common for businesses)
  • DC Fast Charging: 480V+, 100-200+ miles in 30 min (highway/retail)

Business Benefits

  • Attract and retain EV-driving customers and tenants
  • Demonstrate environmental leadership
  • Generate revenue from charging fees
  • Increase property value and marketability
  • Meet sustainability and ESG goals

North Carolina Incentives

  • Federal Tax Credit: Up to 30% of costs (max $100K)
  • NC State Tax Credit: Available for qualifying installations
  • Duke Energy Rebates: Up to $4,000 per port
  • Local utility programs and grants
  • VW Settlement funds for public charging

Key Considerations

  • Electrical panel capacity and upgrade costs
  • Parking layout and ADA accessibility
  • Network vs. standalone chargers
  • Payment processing and user access
  • Maintenance and warranty coverage

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about EV charging infrastructure

How much does it cost to install EV charging stations? +

Costs vary widely based on several factors: charger type (Level 2 vs DC Fast), number of stations, electrical infrastructure needs, and site conditions. A typical Level 2 commercial installation ranges from $5,000-$15,000 per port including equipment and installation. However, with available incentives, your net cost can be 30-50% lower. We provide detailed cost estimates during our free consultation.

What incentives are available in North Carolina? +

North Carolina offers multiple incentive programs: Federal tax credits covering up to 30% of costs (Alternative Fuel Infrastructure Tax Credit), Duke Energy and other utility rebates, NC state tax credits for eligible installations, and VW Settlement funds for public charging. We help you identify and apply for all applicable programs to maximize your savings.

How long does installation typically take? +

Timeline varies by project complexity. A typical installation involves: 1-2 weeks for site assessment and design, 2-4 weeks for permitting and utility coordination, and 1-3 days for physical installation. Total timeframe is usually 6-12 weeks from project kickoff to operational chargers. We can expedite critical projects when needed.

Do I need to upgrade my electrical service? +

It depends on your existing electrical capacity and the number/type of chargers you plan to install. Our site assessment includes a thorough electrical evaluation. Many properties have sufficient capacity for 2-4 Level 2 chargers without upgrades. For larger installations, we explore load management systems and phased approaches to minimize upgrade costs.

Should I choose networked or standalone chargers? +

Networked chargers offer remote monitoring, payment processing, usage data, and user access control through a cloud-based platform. They're ideal for revenue generation and customer access. Standalone chargers are simpler and lower-cost, best for employee-only use or free charging programs. We help you choose based on your specific business goals and budget.

What ongoing maintenance is required? +

EV chargers are relatively low-maintenance. Regular tasks include periodic inspection of cables and connectors, software updates (for networked stations), and occasional cleaning. Most manufacturers offer 3-year warranties, with extended coverage available. We can coordinate maintenance services and provide troubleshooting support to ensure maximum uptime.
